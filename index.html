Pinocchio

(Carlo Collodi)

An anthology of stories by the main Chinese science fiction writer Liu Cixin. The texts included in the collection were written from 1999 to 2017 - it turns out to be a kind of review of 18 years of writing. From this angle, one can trace not only the evolution of the author, but also the development of Cixin’s key idea - the relationship of the small and insignificant (or only seemingly so) with the grandiose and eternal. Cixin is inspired by the best examples of classical fiction - books by Arthur C. Clarke, Philip K. Dick, Isaac Asimov, Frank Herbert - gracefully interweaving oriental flavor and her thoughts on how progress will change a person individually and society as a whole.
